Sir Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya (1861-1962) was an Indian civil engineer, statesman, and administrator who made significant contributions to India's infrastructure and industrial development: Engineering projects: He was responsible for many engineering projects, including the Krishna Raja Sagara dam in Mandya, which helped convert wasteland into fertile land. He also served as the special consultant engineer for the Hyderabad town flood control scheme. Industrialization and education: He is credited with accelerating industrialization and education in India. Block system of irrigation: He introduced the block system of irrigation in the Deccan Canals to control water misuse. Awards: He was appointed a Companion of the Order of the Indian Empire (CIE) and later knighted KCIE for his services to British India. In 1955, he was awarded the Bharat Ratna by the Government of India. Birthplace: He was born in Muddenahalli, a village in the state of Karnataka. Education: He studied engineering at the College of Engineering in Pune and graduated as a civil engineer in 1884. Legacy: His birthday, September 15, is celebrated as Engineer's Day in India, Sri Lanka, and Tanzania. The Visvesvaraya Industrial and Technological Museum in Bangalore is named in his honor.
